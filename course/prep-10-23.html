    Section Resources for 10/23

    Objectives

    For the content of this class, you will be expected to be able to do the following:
    🔗
    • Explain why the function \(f(x) = b^x\) has an inverse when \(b > 0\) and \(b \ne 1\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    • Know that the inverse of \(f(x)\) in this case is \(f^{-1}(x) = \log_b(x)\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    • Use the definition of the logarithm functions to compute \(\log_b(x)\) for certain values of \(x\) and \(b\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    • Use your understanding of logarithms to solve equations involving exponential and logarithm functions.
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗

    In-Class Activities 39. Introduction to Logarithms.

    In the first three problems, compute the given quantity.
    🔗
    1. \(\displaystyle \log_3(27)\)
      🔗
      🔗
    2. \(\displaystyle \log_2(64)\)
      🔗
      🔗
    3. \(\displaystyle \log_{11}(121)\)
      🔗
      🔗
    4. Suppose \(\log_a(x)=3\) and \(\log_a(y)=5\text{.}\)
      1. Find an expression for \(x\) and \(y\) in terms of \(a\text{.}\)
        🔗
        🔗
      2. Use what you found in (a) to compute both \(\log_a(xy)\) and \(\log_a \left( \frac{x}{y} \right)\)
        🔗
        🔗
      3. Compute \(\log_a \left( x^6 \right)\) and \(\log_a(y^4)\text{.}\)
        🔗
        🔗

    In-Class Activities 40. Solving Logarithm Equations.

    Solve the following equations/problems.
    🔗
    1. \(\displaystyle 3\log_4(x) = 18\)
      🔗
      🔗
    2. \(\displaystyle 5^{6x}=3\)
      🔗

    3. \(\displaystyle 20 \cdot 6^{2x}-1=15\)
      🔗
      🔗
    4. (Not on quiz) Use the graph of \(y=\left( \frac12 \right)^x\) to draw the graph of \(y=\log_{\frac12}(x)\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
